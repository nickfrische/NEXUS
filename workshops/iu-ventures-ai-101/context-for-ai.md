## The Workshop Philosophy (What BPN Is Teaching)

### The System Being Built
Not a chat tool. A persistent AI workspace that:
- Has a master briefing file that loads automatically and tells Claude who the user is
- Has a database of relationships (Jon's alumni files), documents (Olivia's diligence templates), and references (Tony's investment criteria) that Claude reads before doing anything
- Is connected to the tools they already use (Airtable, Notion, Outlook, Zoom) via connectors so Claude reads live data, not copies
- Has skills -- pre-built automations triggered by a single command that run full workflows

### The Three-Stage Model
Stage 1: User explains themselves every time. Structured prompts. Good output. Slow.
Stage 2: Context baked into the system. Claude knows who you are before you type anything.
Stage 3: User just works. Short commands. Full output. Claude already knows everything else.

Phil is building Stage 2 today. Stage 3 is the destination.

### The Approval Layer (Critical Concept)
AI gets right up to the line. Nothing goes out without human review.

Jon's introduction is drafted, formatted, ready to send. Olivia's diligence first-pass is structured and sourced. Tony's deal brief is pulled and prioritized. Nothing leaves until the human reviews it and approves. The human stops doing the 80 percent that surrounds the decision. They do the decision.

This is not optional design. It is the architecture. Any AI action that touches an external party (email, message, document sent out) requires Nick or the relevant user to approve before it executes.

### The Self-Learning Dimension
The system expands as it is used. Every meeting note added to Jon's alumni database improves the next introduction. Every diligence report improves Olivia's template. Every context file Tony builds makes the next deal brief more accurate.

The arc:
- Today: you do the work, AI assists
- Next month: AI drafts, you review and approve
- Six months: AI identifies what needs to be done, surfaces recommendations, prepares the work, waits for approval

### Core Prompting Philosophy
1. Ask Claude how to approach something before starting -- not "do this," but "what is the best approach and what are the potential issues? Wait for my input before proceeding."
2. Ask for options -- "give me three ways to approach this and the tradeoffs of each. I will pick one."
3. Humble curiosity -- the posture of the best AI users. Not "tell me the answer." Not "do it for me." "Here is what I know. What am I missing? What is the best way in?"
4. Iterate -- the first response is a draft, not a final answer. Push back, refine, adjust.
