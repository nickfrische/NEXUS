### The Core Problem: Equipment Tracking

**Equipment types at risk:**
- Multi-million dollar boilers, HVAC, pumps, electrical gear
- Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs) and sensitive electronics

**Scale of loss:**
- Arizona project (~$1B total project value): ~$1M in equipment lost over lifecycle
- Lebanon, IN data center (~$10B): used as a reference example of scale — NOT an Atlas project. Illustrates the magnitude of the problem class Atlas works in.

**Storage locations (chaotic by nature):**
- On-site: job trailers, Connexes (shipping containers), inside partially completed buildings — often poorly labeled
- Third-party warehouses: within ~20 miles of site, quality varies widely; Atlas/owner/GC do NOT own them

**Environmental risk (critical for sensitive electronics):**
- VFDs designed for conditioned environments (68°F, controlled humidity)
- Phoenix example: warehouses promise 68°F but can reach 120°F in summer → VFDs get damaged/dead after 6 months
- Damage is impossible to prove after the fact — no temperature logging, no audit trail

**Accountability gaps across handoffs:**
- OEM/Manufacturer → responsible until product is on the truck
- Shipping company → responsible from pickup to initial delivery
- Owner/GC → responsible after receiving at site or warehouse
- Once in third-party warehouse or on-site: accountability becomes "very fuzzy" — no one can prove when/where damage occurred

**Current tracking method:** Excel / Google Sheets — manual, multiple spreadsheets, painful, inconsistent, error-prone

**Business impact:**
- Direct: Hundreds of thousands to millions in lost/damaged equipment per project
- Indirect: Project delays when equipment can't be found or has to be reordered
- Labor: Multiple full-time asset/inventory managers on mega projects just trying to locate things

---

### Austin's Vision for the Solution

1. Location tracking — know where every piece of equipment is at all times: in transit, on-site, in warehouse, within job site zones
2. Environmental monitoring — temperature, humidity, pressure logging to prove storage conditions were met (or weren't) — defensible data for insurance/claims
3. Chain-of-custody / audit trail — documented at every handoff from manufacturer to final installation; eliminates finger-pointing, enables insurance claims
4. UX target: "Find My iPhone" simplicity — open an app, see where the VFD is, go get it

**Why it's feasible now:** AirTag-level hardware is mainstream and cheap. The job site just hasn't adopted it yet. This is a gap waiting to be filled.
