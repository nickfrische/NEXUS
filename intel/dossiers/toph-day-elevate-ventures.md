## TOPH'S STRATEGIC ADVICE TO BPN

### 1. Go One Vertical Deep
Pick ONE vertical, go as deep as possible. That's what VCs want to see. Horizontal plays don't scale.

### 2. Manufacturing / Data Efficiency = Best Vertical
**His thesis:**
- Manufacturing companies have grossly inefficient supply chains, fleet, inventory, production
- Still carry good margins despite the inefficiency
- A player who cleans their data can cut 5% of costs across the board
- That 5% is enough to push inefficient competitors out of the market
- **Real-world example:** Toph's buddy went to North Carolina, bought the companies he could acquire, then used operational efficiency to push the ones he couldn't buy out of the market over several years

### 3. BPN Positioning Play
Go into a company like DEAM. Capture ONE data point / inefficiency at a time. Build trust. Expand to full-scale efficiency maximization over time. Don't try to boil the ocean on Day 1.

### 4. HVAC as Potential Third Vertical
HVAC companies have zero tech moat. Whoever has clean data wins. Worth exploring alongside manufacturing.

### 5. The Fear/Urgency CEO Pitch
Build a one-pager: "Are you doing XYZ? Here's what the market is doing."
- CEOs don't have time to scope industry trends
- Use competitive fear and urgency to move them to action
- Door-opener, not a closing tool

### 6. Loosely-Scoped Engagement Model ($5K/month)
Toph's proven playbook (used at Elevate itself):
- 3-month engagement at ~$5K/month
- Loosely defined scope: go in, find inefficiencies, build automations, clean data
- Low barrier to entry → opens door to larger downstream engagement
