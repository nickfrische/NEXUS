## Background

Tony has been President and CEO of IU Ventures since August 2008, making him one of the longest-tenured university venture leaders in the country. He is also Associate VP at IU in the Office of VP for Government Relations and Economic Engagement, and President and CEO of Indiana University Research and Technology Corporation (IURTC).

He is a triple IU alumnus: B.S. from Kelley School of Business, J.D. from Maurer School of Law, M.B.A. from Butler. Native of Madison, Indiana.

Before IU Ventures, he served as Executive Director and General Counsel of the Indiana 21st Century Research and Technology Fund -- a $140 million state fund that provided early-stage investments and managed collaborative research partnerships. He was also appointed by Governor Mitch Daniels in January 2005 to the Indiana State Budget Agency to help manage the $26 billion annual state budget. He has been navigating public-private capital and innovation policy since 1997.

## His NSF Role (Critical Context)

In 2024, Tony was appointed to the advisory board of the NSF's "Industries of Ideas" project. His stated mission: help design federal measurement systems to track the economic impact of AI and EV R&D investments on regional economies -- measuring how idea industries form, how talent flows from federal research into jobs and firms.

His public quote on the initiative: "America's economic competitiveness depends on its ability to accelerate high-impact research discoveries, move ideas to the marketplace and expand our STEM workforce. This project will generate a new, data-driven system to directly measure the effects of research investments on boosting firms and talent pipelines, as well as the development of emerging technology areas."

This makes him one of the few university leaders thinking about AI at a federal policy level, not just a campus level.
