## KEY STRATEGIC INSIGHTS MATT GAVE NICK (for BrotherLink)

### Sales Strategy
1. **Do NOT sell to nationals.** Red tape kills deals. Chapters resent top-down mandates. ("Like your parents telling you to do the dishes.") Always sell to chapters directly.
2. **The Car Analogy.** Find a problem worth solving — one people HAVE to solve, not a nice-to-have.
3. **Always sell in threes.** Frat guys can follow three things. Never more.
4. **Pitch presidents on THEIR goals.** Ask "is this something you want to change?" before pitching anything.
5. **Referrals are big. Conferences are not.** Everyone's pitching the same thing at conferences.
6. **Give product away as demo/pilot — but there needs to be skin in the game.** Free with no stakes gets no attention.

### Messaging & Positioning
7. **"Nostalgia to network"** — Matt called it the hook. Alliteration helps.
8. **What really resonates:** "You're not selling a tool — you're helping chapters spend time with cool alumni, not the 'boner about the house' stereotypes." Find the 70% of cool alumni who aren't coming back because nobody's reaching out.
9. **Curt Cignetti analogy.** Change the culture around networking — reframe it as connecting with cool people.
10. **Don't over-invest in brand right now.** Focus on solving the problem and building the product.
11. **Matt doesn't love the name "BrotherLink."** Encouraged Nick, Blake, and Phil to rethink the name.

### Delivery Model
12. **Train the chapter, don't just sell them software.** Run a workshop, solve their problem of building a living alumni database, then give them the software to maintain it.
13. **Get undergrads bought in first.** If undergrads love the system, they sell it to grad brothers themselves.
14. **The Pig Dinner Problem.** Undergrads don't want to spend 6 hours with 40-50-year-old alumni. Fix the format, not just the platform.

### Market Segmentation
15. **Three chapter types ("Lemonade Networks"):**
    - **(a) Dormant Alumni** — No network. Build from scratch.
    - **(b) Degenerate / No Trust** — Chapter unstable; people won't donate or engage.
    - **(c) Middle** — The real market.
16. **FIJI IU Zeta is on the far RIGHT of the bell curve.** Focus BrotherLink on the **middle of the bell curve**, not outliers.

### Pricing & Business Model
17. **Tiered pricing.** More features / higher price for stronger chapters. Base model for smaller ones.
18. **Don't expect profit year 1 or 2.** "Faithfulness over fruitfulness." Build the community first.
